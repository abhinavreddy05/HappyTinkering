Our Aim

Our Vision

Our founder found inspiration for the venture in Prof. Christensen's theories and Prof. Vanbever's teachings.

The vision of Happy Tinkering is to nurture creativity and curiosity, foster an innovative and entrepreneurial mindset, and build 21st-century STE(A)M skills in the adolescent youth of developing countries. The social venture will help communities create homegrown innovation and entrepreneurship ecosystems by designing and implementing strategic initiatives and programs.

Our Methodology

In our pursuit of fostering vibrant innovation ecosystems in resource-constrained environments of the Global South, we employ a comprehensive and strategic approach.

Our methodology involves a meticulous study of causal factors that drive positive change. By identifying mutually exclusive yet collectively exhaustive elements, we aim to shape the foundation of impactful transformation. Our commitment extends beyond research, as we leverage these insights to develop robust frameworks.

We believe in democratizing knowledge through academic research, teaching, and publishing, ensuring that our findings contribute significantly to the enhancement of human capital. Join us as we embark on a journey to catalyze sustainable innovation and empower communities worldwide.

Mr. Ronak Jogeshwar

Former IL-ATL, at AIM and Harvard Alumni
Founder - Happy Tinkering

Ronak is a tinkerer, innovator, and cook. His making journey started with Maker’s Asylum maker space, and a year later, he helped establish the first 10 maker spaces in schools known as the Atal Tinkering Labs, currently a movement of more than 10,000 schools.

At 25, he joined NITI Aayog’s Atal Innovation Mission and is the youngest member of its founding team. A first-generation student, he started working at 12, fixing photocopier machines with his father to make ends meet.

His early experiences of operating in a resource-constrained environment led him to Harvard University, where his research focus is on how to create innovation and entrepreneurship ecosystems for developing nations.

He believes in democratizing an action-oriented Innovation culture wherever he goes, guided by a trident of inclusivity, empathy, and impact. And he is always on the hunt for opportunities to practice his making!

Ronak is bringing his passion for Tinkering and Making, a mission to democratize Innovation and entrepreneurship, through a vocation of creating solid frameworks from his schools of experience, by forging himself into a profession guided by the ‘what’, ‘how’, and ‘why’ story of the Tinkering movement of India.

His initiative, Happy Tinkering’s purpose is to design, deliver, demonstrate, and democratize impactful innovation strategies and programs across communities to foster a mindset change.
